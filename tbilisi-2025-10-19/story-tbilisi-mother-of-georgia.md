### 3. The Symbolism Unlocked (1:15-2:15)

Here's the thing about Georgia, and I mean this. This country greets you with wine and warmth like nowhere else I've been. Gaumarjos at every table, strangers inviting you to feast, that ridiculous, overwhelming hospitality that makes you feel guilty for not speaking Georgian fluently.

But cross them? Threaten what they love? You get the sword.

That's what she represents. That's what this whole statue is screaming without saying a word. Wine for friends. Steel for enemies. And Georgians will tell you - straight up, as it is - that's exactly who they are.

The sculptor, Elguja Amashukeli, he made her in 1958 for Tbilisi's 1500th anniversary. Started as wood, can you believe that? Wooden statue. But people loved her so much they covered her in aluminum in 1963, rebuilt her entirely in 1997. She was supposed to be temporary. She became eternal.

He called her 'Capital.' Georgians said 'nope, she's Kartlis Deda - Mother of Georgia.' And guess what? The official name changed. The people won.
